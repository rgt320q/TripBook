This document explains the features of the Trip Book app and how to use it.

1. Overview
Trip Book is a mobile app designed to help you plan your travels, save locations, create routes, and track your progress during trips.

2. Map Screen & Basic Operations

The main screen of the app is an interactive map.

2.1. Location Pins
- Your Current Location: Displayed as a glowing blue dot on the map.
- Saved Locations: Shown as classic Google Maps-style pins.
  - Color Coding:
    - Locations assigned to a group take on the groupâ€™s color.
    - Locations not assigned to any group appear gray.
    - Locations visited during an active route turn green.

2.2. Adding a New Location
- Long-press anywhere on the map to add a new location.
- You can enter a custom name, description, notes, checklist, and estimated stop duration.

2.3. Go to My Location Button
- Tap the target (my_location) icon at the bottom right to center the map on your current location.

3. Location & Group Management

The app provides dedicated screens to manage your locations and groups efficiently.

3.1. Manage Locations
- Access via the list (list_alt) icon in the top menu.
- All saved locations are listed here.
- You can sort by name or creation date.
- Tap any location to edit its details (name, description, group, notes, etc.).
- Map Integration: Tapping a pin on the map opens this screen and scrolls to the relevant location.

3.2. Manage Groups
- Access via the folder (folder_copy_outlined) icon in the top menu.
- Create, edit, and delete colored groups to categorize your locations.
- When editing a location, you can assign it to one of these groups.

4. Route Planning & Usage

The app allows you to create dynamic routes from your saved locations.

4.1. Creating a Route
- Tap the directions icon in the top menu of the map screen.
- Select from Group: Creates a route using all locations in a selected group.
- Manual Selection: Lets you choose specific locations from the list to build a custom route.
- Once created, the app orders the locations efficiently based on your current position.

4.2. Saving & Loading Routes
- Saving: Tap the save icon on the route summary screen to name and save the current route.
- Loading: Tap the route (route_sharp) icon in the top menu to load previously saved routes.

4.3. Route Summary & Checklist
- Tap the summarize icon in the top menu to view a summary of the current or active route.
- Includes total distance, estimated travel and stop durations.
- Checklist:
  - All items added to locations along the route are combined into a unified checklist.
  - This list is specific to the current trip. Checked items remain marked until the route is closed.
  - Reopening or restarting the same route template resets the checklist (unchecked).
- Special Notes: All notes from locations on the route are displayed together on this screen.

5. Active Route Tracking

When a route is started, the map screen actively tracks your journey.

- Upcoming Path: Untraveled segments are shown in light gray.
- Traveled Path: Segments you've completed turn bright blue permanently.
- Reached Stops: When you get within 50 meters of a stop, its pin turns green permanently.
- Notifications:
  - When approaching a stop, you receive a notification with a Wikipedia summary (if available).
  - If you stay longer than planned at a stop, a time reminder notification is sent.

5.1. Reached Locations History
- Tap the history icon in the top menu to view a list of all locations reached during a route, along with their Wikipedia links.
